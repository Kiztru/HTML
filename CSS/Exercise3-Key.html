Industrial minerals

Industrial minerals are any rock, mineral, or other naturally occurring material of economic value, excluding metals, energy minerals, and gemstones


Why are industrial minerals so essential?

Because your world is made of them
The average American uses about one million pounds of industrial minerals, such as limestone, clay, and aggregate, over the period of a lifetime.

Kuwait University

Examples

  • Silica
  • Alumina
  • Limestone
  • Clays
  • Sand

Limestone

is a common type of carbonate sedimentary rock which is the main source of the material lime. It has two origins, biogenic precipitation from seawater or mechanical transport and deposition of preexisting limestones, forming clastic deposits
